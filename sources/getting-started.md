# Getting Started: Preparation & Reconstitution Guide

Welcome to your research journey. To ensure the best results and maintain the integrity of your products, it is important to follow proper preparation and storage protocols. This guide will walk you through the essentials of getting started.

## Essential Supplies Needed
Before beginning, you will need to purchase the following three items to safely prepare and handle your peptides:

1. **Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water**: This is required to reconstitute the lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptide powder into a liquid solution, ensuring it remains sterile and stable.
2. **Insulin Syringes**: These are necessary for precise measurement, transfer of the BAC water, and accurate research administration.
3. **Sterile Alcohol Pads**: Essential for maintaining a clean environment and sanitizing the vial stoppers before any transfers.

## Understanding Reconstitution
Peptides arrive as a freeze-dried powder to preserve their stability during transit. Reconstitution is simply the process of gently mixing this powder with Bacteriostatic Water to create a usable liquid solution.

*(Please note: The exact amount of BAC water needed varies by peptide. Always refer to the specific guidelines provided on the individual peptide's information page.)*

### Step-by-Step Reconstitution Process

1. **Prepare a Clean Workspace**: Wash your hands thoroughly and lay out your supplies on a clean, sanitized surface.
2. **Sanitize the Vials**: Take a fresh sterile alcohol pad and wipe the rubber stoppers of both the peptide vial and the BAC water vial. Allow them a moment to air dry.
3. **Draw the BAC Water**: Using a new insulin syringe, draw out the exact amount of BAC water required for your specific peptide.
4. **Insert the Water Gently**: Carefully push the needle through the rubber stopper of the peptide vial. **Important:** Angle the needle so the water flows down the inside glass wall of the vial, rather than spraying directly onto the delicate powder.
5. **Dissolve by Rolling**: Once the water is inserted, **do not shake the vial.** Shaking can damage the fragile peptide structures. Instead, slowly and gently roll the vial between your fingers until the powder is fully dissolved and the liquid is completely clear.
6. **Refrigerate Immediately**: As soon as the peptide is reconstituted, it must be stored in the refrigerator. Keeping the liquid cool is critical for maintaining its potency and extending its shelf life.
